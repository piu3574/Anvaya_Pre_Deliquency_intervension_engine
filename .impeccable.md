## Design Context

### Users
The primary users are banking officials and Relationship Managers (RMs). They use this dashboard in a high-stakes, operational context to monitor pre-delinquency risk, triage emerging alerts, and proactively manage customer loan defaults before they happen. The job to be done is quickly identifying high-risk customers, understanding the 'why' behind the risk score (via SHAP drivers), and initiating empathetic outreach.

### Brand Personality
Professional, Trustworthy, Analytical. The tone is clear, objective, and clinical, yet geared toward enabling deeply empathetic customer care rather than hostile collections.

### Aesthetic Direction
A modern, sophisticated, white-and-blue banking dashboard. 
- **Theme**: Light mode.
- **Visual Tone**: Clean, data-dense but not overwhelming, utilizing subtle shadows, distinct color-coded risk bands (Green/Yellow/Red), and smooth gradients. 
- **References**: "Aegis" operational risk command center. Focuses on strong visual hierarchy and clear distinct components (cards, pills, data tables).

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over Clutter**: Operational risk interfaces must prioritize the signal over the noise. Important data (high risk PDs) must immediately draw the eye.
2. **Action-Oriented Insight**: Data shouldn't just be displayed; it must be actionable. Drivers and risk bands should instinctively guide the RM's next step.
3. **Clinical but Empathic**: The UI should feel precise, secure, and rock-solid (banking standard), but the end goal is empathetic intervention.
4. **Resilient Feedback**: System states (like the pipeline running) must be clearly visible through micro-interactions and terminal-style logs so the user always trusts the system is working.
